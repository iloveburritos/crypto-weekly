**THIS WEEK IN CRYPTO**
\- Treasuries surged this week, as weakness in the US job market led to de-risking across stocks, commodities, and cryptocurrencies, with crypto and software companies leading this week's losses. [Link](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-05/us-bonds-rally-as-job-market-angst-backs-wagers-on-fed-rate-cuts).

\- Bitcoin briefly dipped below $60,000 this week, representing a roughly 52% drop since its all-time high of $126,000 in October. [Link](https://fortune.com/2026/02/06/bitcoin-claws-back-above-70000-after-worst-day-since-ftx-crash/).

\- Bitcoin extended losses as the unwinding of leveraged bets and broader market turbulence deepened a selloff that wiped out all of the gains since President Donald Trump's election. [Link](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-05/bitcoin-drops-below-70-000-as-forced-deleveraging-accelerates).

\- The Crypto Fear and Greed Index hit "Extreme Fear" territory, its lowest level in 42 months since the collapse of the UST Terra / Luna stablecoin. [Link](https://decrypt.co/357159/crypto-sentiment-sinks-lowest-level-3-5-years). [Index](https://alternative.me/crypto/fear-and-greed-index/).

\- Senator Cynthia Lummis urged banks to embrace stablecoins, calling them a faster, cheaper payment rail, after CLARITY Act talks stalled due to pushback from banks on stablecoin rewards. [Link](https://decrypt.co/357157/senator-lummis-banks-embrace-stablecoins-clarity-act-stalemate).

\- With its significant price decline this week, bitcoin has returned roughly 73% since early 2021, trailing gold at 164% and the S&P 500 at 75%. [Link](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-05/bitcoin-trails-s-p-500-nasdaq-100-gold-over-five-year-horizon).

\- Thirty-day volatility in gold hit 44%, the highest level since the 2008 financial crisis, surpassing the roughly 39% for bitcoin. [Link](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-02/gold-volatility-tops-bitcoin-in-wildest-price-swings-since-2008).

\- Y Combinator, Silicon Valley's most prominent accelerator, will allow its startup cohorts to receive their $500,000 of funding in USDC stablecoins. [Link](https://fortune.com/2026/02/03/famed-startup-incubator-y-combinator-to-let-founders-receive-funds-in-stablecoins/). [Link](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/03/yc-startups-can-now-recieve-investment-in-stablecoin/).

\- Ethereum co-founder Vitalik Buterin said the original idea of Layer 2’s acting as secure extensions of the ethereum mainnet had failed to properly materialize, and instead focus should continue towards improving scaling of mainnet with Layer 2’s pivoting towards specialization. [Link](https://unchainedcrypto.com/vitalik-buterin-calls-for-rethink-of-ethereum-layer-2s-as-native-rollups-take-center-stage/). [Link](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2026/02/05/vitalik-buterin-blasts-ethereum-copypasta-l2-chains-says-the-rollup-excuse-is-fading). [Tweet](https://x.com/VitalikButerin/status/2019341766407725170?s=20).

\- ENS Labs, the foundation behind Ethereum Name Service and the .eth domains, is cancelling the launch of its own Layer 2 blockchain and will deploy its upcoming ENSv2 update on the ethereum mainnet. [Link](https://www.theblock.co/post/388932/ens-labs-scraps-namechain-l2-shifts-ensv2-fully-ethereum-mainnet).

\- Digital Asset Treasuries are largely down on their holdings, with Strategy (BTC) and BitMine (ETH) holding the biggest paper losses of $9.2 billion and $8.4 billion respectively. [Link](https://decrypt.co/357130/crypto-treasury-underwater-bitcoin-ethereum-solana-dive).

\- Multicoin co-founder Kyle Samani is stepping down from his role at the crypto venture fund, to "explore new areas of technology". Samani and Multicoin were early Solana investors. [Link](https://www.theblock.co/post/388561/multicoin-founder-kyle-samani-steps-back-vc-firm-continue-advocate-solana). The primary suspect is a relative of the primary custodian, Virginia based firm Command Services and Support, who won the contract in 2024.
