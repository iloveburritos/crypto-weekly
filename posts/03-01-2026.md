**THIS WEEK IN CRYPTO**
\- Bitcoin and cryptocurrencies traded erratically after US-Israel strikes killed Iran's supreme leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei as traders await direction from traditional markets on Monday. [Link](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-01/bitcoin-btc-recovery-looks-tepid-as-iran-strikes-stir-uncertainty).

\- Bitcoin was down roughly 24% in February, its worst monthly performance since June 2022 when TerraUSD imploded and triggered a daisy chain of failures across the ecosystem. [Link](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-24/bitcoin-btc-heads-for-worst-month-since-june-2022-crypto-winter?srnd=phx-crypto).

\- Bitcoin ETFs saw roughly $1 billion in inflows this week, reversing 5 consecutives weeks of outflows totaling $4.3 billion. [Link](https://etfdb.com/coinshares-crypto-etf-hub/coinshares-channel/bitcoin-febrility-persists-floor-forming/#:~:text=ETF%20flows:%20five%20consecutive%20weeks,ETF%20launch%20in%20early%202024.). [Link](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/202626/bitcoin-resumes-slide-after-quick-pullback-in-risk-appetite).

\- Quantitative trading firm Jane Street was sued for alleged insider trading by the administrator winding up Terraform Labs, the company behind the Terra / Luna algorithmic stablecoin which lost $40 billion in value in 2022 when its stablecoin depegged. According to the lawsuit, Jane Street obtained non-public information through a former intern's back-channel communications with Terraform insiders to front-run trades that hastened the de-pegging of the stablecoin. [Link](https://www.wsj.com/finance/currencies/jane-street-accused-of-insider-trading-that-helped-collapse-terraform-659e6993). [Link](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-02-26/jane-street-faces-the-wrath-of-crypto-natives-nursing-old-wounds). [Link](https://decrypt.co/358936/terraform-estate-sues-jane-street-trades-tied-2022-collapse).

\- Shares of USDC stablecoin issuer Circle climbed roughly 30% following Q4 earnings of $770 million, up 77% year-over-year. CEO Jeremy Allaire said USDC now accounts for almost 50% of stablecoin transaction volumes, up from a third in the prior quarter. [Link](https://www.theblock.co/post/391275/circle-shares-25-earnings-beat-allaire-usdc-transactions-50-share).

\- Morgan Stanley applied for a new trust bank charter to custody digital assets and support client investment activities including staking. [Link](https://www.forbes.com/sites/jasonbrett/2026/02/27/8-trillion-morgan-stanley-quietly-files-for-national-trust-charter/). [Link](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-27/morgan-stanley-applies-for-bank-charter-to-custody-crypto-assets).

\- The Office of the Comptroller of the Currency proposed rules that would restrict companies from launching branded stablecoins through white-label platforms and offering rewards tied to them. The rules are aimed at stablecoin-as-a-service platforms operated by firms like Paxos, Stripe's Bridge, and Anchorage. [Link](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-26/federal-baregulator-moves-to-restrict-us-stablecoin-rewards).

\- A federal judge in New York denied Binance's motion to compel arbitration in a class action alleging the exchange sold unregistered securities to US investors. [Link](https://www.theblock.co/post/391662/judge-blocks-binance-from-pushing-class-action-suits-over-token-sales-into-arbitration).

\- Barclays is exploring the use of blockchain technology for handling internal payment processes and tokenizing deposits. [Link](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-27/barclays-considers-blockchain-platform-for-payments-deposits?srnd=phx-crypto).

\- Decentralized lending protocol Aave is facing governance tensions ahead of a $51 million funding vote which would merge developer Aave Labs with the Foundation. DAO holders are questioning roughly $86 million in past funding for Aave and asking for greater transparency before tokenholders approve the largest proposal in the protocol's history. [Link](https://www.theblock.co/post/391193/aave-goverispute-intensifies-as-aci-founder-publishes-audit-of-aave-labs-ahead-of-51m-funding-vote).

\- South Korea's National Tax Service revealed the recovery phrase of a seized crypto wallet in a public press release photo, leading to a 'hack' of $4.8 million that was in the wallet. [Link](https://www.theblock.co/post/391578/south-korea-tax-service-reveals-crypto-wallet-recovery-phrase-report). [Link](https://decrypt.co/359404/ethereum-tokens-swiped-returned-south-korean-tax-service).

\- Minnesota lawmakers are considering banning crypto ATMs following a rise in reported scams targeted at the elderly. The state saw 70 kiosk-related complaints last year totalling $540,000 in reported losses. [Link](https://www.theblock.co/post/391541/minnesota-introduces-bill-to-ban-crypto-kiosks).

\- NFT marketplace Magic Eden said it was ending support for Ethereum and Bitcoin assets to focus solely on the Solana ecosystem. [Link](https://decrypt.co/359422/magic-eden-pulls-plug-bitcoin-ethereum-doubles-down-solana).

\- The UK's top financial regulator chose four crypto firms, including neobank Revolut, to participate in a risk-free regulatory sandbox that will inform how the agency shapes stablecoin rules later this year. [Link](https://decrypt.co/359123/uk-selects-firms-stablecoin-regulatory-sandbox-revolut).

\- A solo Bitcoin miner defied the odds by finding a solo block reward, earning 3.125 BTC worth around $200,000. [Link](https://decrypt.co/359005/solo-bitcoin-miner-wins-200k-block-reward-renting-hash-power).

\- The Ethereum Foundation staked around $3.8 million worth of ETH as part of previously announced treasury operations. Over time, the Foundation aims to stake around $127 million worth of ETH, with native rewards going back to the organization to fund operations. [Link](https://decrypt.co/358981/ethereum-foundation-staking-treasury-vitalik-buterin-sales).
